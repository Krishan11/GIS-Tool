Hanno

Hanno the Great was a politician and military leader of the 4th century BC.His got a title of princeps Cathaginiensium. It is considered more likely that the title signifies first among equals, rather than being a title of nobility or royalty. His rival Suniatus was called the potentissimus Poenorum, or "the most powerful of the Carthaginians", in the year 368. Several years later Suniatus was accused of high treason (for correspondence with Syracuse) and was executed. In 367 Hanno the Great commanded a fleet of 200 ships which won a decisive naval victory over the Greeks of Sicily. His victory effectively blocked the plans of Dionysius I of Syracuse to attack Lilybaeum, a city allied to Carthage in western Sicily.

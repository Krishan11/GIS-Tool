Battle of Cape Ecnomus

The Battle of Cape Ecnomus was a naval confrontation between the fleets of Carthage and the the Roman Republic took place in 256 BC at Sicily during the First Punic war. This battle was considered as the largest naval battles of ancient Mediterranean world as there were about 300 rowers and 120 combat troops per ship involved in this battle.The Roman Republic was led by Marcus Atilius Regulus and supported by Lucius Manlius Vulso ,and the Carthage were led by Hanno the Great.The battle Resulted in Roman Victory with 24 ships sunk from Romans side as compared to 30 ships in addition to 65 ships that were captured by the Romans.After the battle, the Romans landed in Sicily for repairs and to rest the crews. The prows of the captured Carthaginian ships were sent to Rome to adorn the rostra of the Forum.
